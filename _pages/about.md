Welcome to my personal website !! I am currently a postdoctoral researcher for [Patterns of Conflict Emergence](https://forecastlab.org) project at [Trinity College Dublin](https://www.tcd.ie/Political_Science/). My research and teaching interests center on international rivalry, foreign policy, and public opinion. While studying these topics, I employ quantitative methods including survey experiments, computational text analysis, and longitudinal survey analysis.

The central motivation of my research is to advance understanding of the microfoundations of international rivalries in East Asia. Despite repeated attempts at cooperation between rival states in the region, many individuals continue to hold negative perceptions of adversaries and oppose conciliatory policies. My research examines these socio-psychological barriers that sustain this resistance to peace among East Asian citizens. Relatedly, my two published works at [Conflict Management and Peace Science](https://doi.org/10.1177/07388942251395480) and [Journal of Contemporary China](https://doi.org/10.1080/10670564.2025.2549103) show how identity-based biases shape public interpretations of adversaries’ cooperative gestures, often neutralizing or even reversing their intended peace-promoting effects. Also, I have several working projects on the broader political impacts on public opinion on rapprochement in East Asia.

Furthermore, my research expands to foreign intervention in civil wars and online violence during electoral campaigns. About further details about my research, please see my [CV](/files/CV_Han_Apr2026.pdf) or contact me via email ([jhan@tcd.ie](mailto:jhan@tcd.ie)).
